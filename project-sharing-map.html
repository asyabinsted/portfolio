Sharing Map:
Mobile App for Community Sharing

Category: Mobile App, Community Platform, Social Impact
Services: UX Research, UX/UI Design, Mobile Design, Prototyping, Usability Testing
All details are anonymized and used solely for portfolio display.

A mobile application that connects neighbors to share resources, reduce waste, and build stronger communities through local sharing networks.

This case study showcases a community-focused mobile app designed to promote sustainable living and neighborhood connections. The app enables users to share items, borrow tools, and participate in local sharing events.

Challenge

Modern communities face challenges with resource waste, social isolation, and lack of local connections. Many people own items they rarely use while others need access to those same resources. Traditional sharing methods are often inconvenient, unsafe, or non-existent in many neighborhoods.

Research

I conducted user interviews and surveys to understand community sharing behaviors, trust factors, and technology adoption patterns. The research revealed key insights about safety concerns, convenience preferences, and the importance of local community building.

Design Process

I developed a user-centered design approach focusing on trust, safety, and ease of use. The design process included wireframing, prototyping, and iterative testing to create an intuitive sharing experience.

Key Features

The app includes features for item sharing, community events, user verification, and local discovery. Each feature was designed to build trust and encourage community participation.

Prototype

I created interactive prototypes to test the user experience and gather feedback. The prototypes demonstrated the complete user journey from onboarding to successful sharing interactions.

Results

The design successfully addressed community sharing challenges by creating a trusted, user-friendly platform. User testing showed high engagement rates and positive feedback on the sharing experience.
