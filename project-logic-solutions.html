I designed the end-to-end shipment management platform, covering shipment operations, tracking, and collaboration with all parties involved.

The new TMS centralizes logistics operations, gives a clear overview of shipments, reduces repeated data entry, streamlines collaboration between agents and customers, and shortens onboarding time for new users.

Logic Solutions offers a platform to logistics companies that automates shipping processes, providing real-time analytics and workflow management to enhance the shipping experience.

My role: Product UX / UI Designer

Project duration: Mar'24-Sep'25

Team: 1 Product owner, 1 Project manager, 2 Frontend developers, 4 Backend developers

Challenge & Objective

The platform in use at the time had started as an accounting tool and gradually grew to support multiple departments. Over time, it became powerful and feature-rich, but also complex and hard to use.

While it worked reliably and offered many functions, it had major limitations. It was built in the early 2000s on technology that made changes difficult and restricted design improvements. Navigation was confusing, workflows were manual, tasks had to be repeated, and information was scattered. New users required special training that took a long time to understand how everything worked, and tracking shipments was slow and error-prone.

My task was to design a platform for all agents in the logistics process, with the goal of easing onboarding on a complex system. The aim was to help new users get started quickly, understand workflows easily, reduce repeated work, and make information simple to find and manage.
